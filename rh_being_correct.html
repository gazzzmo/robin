Just being correct will do

The analysis of 774 ABC Melbourne by former staffer Tracie Winch presents a curious view of what many committed listeners hear as a radio station in serious decline. While it may suit station manager Tony Walker to claim that Jon Faine possesses a "fiercely strong intellectual ability", many listeners note other interesting attributes. As a person dealing daily with current events, Faine often appears not to be aware of significant happenings in areas we expect him to know about. Among the most glaring examples are those pertaining to industrial relations. Another problem since Sue Howard became manager of ABC radio is the fervent push to "political correctness". This is most noticeable in the rush to promote women into leading slots as "personalities" when they appear not to be best qualified. Winch also avoided any analysis of the shoddy standard of panel work and production on 774 these days. Or the boring chit-chat from callers allowed to ask X, Y and Z presenter "How are you today?" Not a criticism of the fourth-rate calls put to air from telephone users who sound as if they are calling via a jam tin. Finally, 774 listeners are eagerly awaiting the day when news bulletins can be put to air with the correct sound bites inserted at the right place. Maybe 774 is the daily count for the number of monumental errors the staff put to air!

Robin F. Howells, Nunawading

The Age Green Guide - June 28, 2001
