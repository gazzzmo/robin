By ROBIN HOWELLS
in Daylesford

CARTOONIST Michael Leunig, Raymond Cloonan and Stefan Szonyi, all of Daylesford Victoria, were charged in the Daylesford court late last week with exhibiting obscene figures and offensive behavior.

   Magistrate A. G. McCallum was told by the police prosecutor and informant sergeant D. Hume that around 9.45pm on december 31 1975 he was in attendance at the Daylesford new year gala procession which took place in Daylesford's (main) Vincent street, and observed a four ton truck with brush or leaves to four feet high around the tray and two cutout human forms depicting a naked male and female. The float was called "Fun in the Forest".

   Questioned by defence barrister Tim Morris, Hume said: "I can't recall anyone reacting to this float with laughter."

   After further evidence from prosecution and defence witnesses including Leunig, magistrate McCallum suggested that the police withdraw the charge of exhibiting an obscene figure. 

He then placed Leunig, Cloonan and Szonyi on good behavior bonds for one year on the offensive behavior charges. They were also ordered to pay $50 each into the court poor box.

For more on Leunig and the law, see Outsight with John Hepworth, p. 744.

 

Nation Review - may 7-13 1976 - page 728

 

Author's Note: This heavily subedited version of the original story filed was drastically cut by Richard Walsh before publication. The two cutout forms can be seen in the accompanying photograph. They were seized during the parade by the police and tendered as exhibits during the case in the Daylesford court. The photo was taken illegally in the court by a journalist (now deceased) using a tiny spy camera. He leaned over the visitors' balcony just before the luncheon adjournment to capture the only images of the offending figures.
