Samantha Dawe

God, Christ it's the
Woman's day lensman

HELLO DARLINGS, I've just managed to attend the glittering world premiere of Margaret Fink's production of The removalists. There were so many stars and top people at the premiere it was a pleasure to be in attendance with so many of her relatives. Margaret must come from a big close family.

   Even as I arrived at Hoyt's cinema centre in Melbourne's Bourke street I realised it was going to be another wunderbar evening. Woman's day's photographer was actually waiting on the footpath to record my arrival. Darling lad, Les.

   Most of the beautiful people were standing around in the foyer hoping that they would be noticed by the horrid social writers for those common mass circulation sheets (God knows why). They didnt dare go inside until they were seen and the show was 20 minutes late starting. V. tiring.

   Trying to make my way through the foyer I noticed that delightful chap Harold de Marigny. Harold is quite an identity in Melbourne because he always seems to be wearing a panama hat. I've heard it suggested that he even wears it under the shower. Naturally, I can't vouch for such an amazing story.

   Tenants of the housing commission of Victoria who are behind in their rent might need to watch out with the nasty tips Harold the Hat may have picked up from The removalists . . . because harold is their public relations officer.

   Although the invitations specified "black tie" I was pleased to see that jeweller  Matcham Skipper from Montsalvat country came dressed in casual skivvy, slacks and Eltham mudcaked boots. That man is unbelievably masculine.

   Didnt see my dear friend Vi Greenandahalf MBE. I can only assume that she wasnt invited so that Margaret Fink could invite two more of her cousins to see the film. Pity, because I was looking forward to more of Vi's witty repartee. She was absolutely brilliant at the premiere of Scobie Malone last fortnight. She told us how she came home from GTV9 the other day with "drippy drawers". Isnt she priceless? She got an MBE last week. Bravo Vi!

   Waved to Alan Finney, late of Flicks fame with ABC TV. Alan looked spiffy in a rusty red two piece suit and a bold brown & white striped shirt open at the collar. Hope MF didnt notice he didnt have a black tie. I suppose if you work for the Roadshow/Village theatre group as Alan does you dont have to care what you wear to a Fink feature.

   After Margaret's movie had successfully set us ablaze, we all trouped across to the Southern Cross hotel for a champers supper. Isnt MF lucky that her husband Leon - who wasnt one of the Sydney "Push" - controls the firm that owns the Southern Cross? As I said: a big, close family.

   Entering the Southern Cross ballroom I knew I was at a truly glittering occasion. My dears, the chandelier has to be seen to be believed. Brilliant. And all those super trendy Kentia palms seem to absolutely thrive on the dregs of so much champagne.

   MF's man Leon certainly didnt stint on the champers. It was flowing like water and like everyone else I did get a bit tiddly. But not before I noted who the best dressed people in the ballroom were.

   It really was Margaret Fink's night. She stole the attention with her beautifully cut long black gown with blue embroidery. Simple but so chic and it moulded her figure lovingly. Margaret's daughter Hannah, although just 12, was a close runner up. Hannah wore a batik dress with a swirl skirt and looked to be enjoying herself immensely. After Hannah, I couldnt help noting Margaret's cousin May. May so fey. May so gay. May with her black dress with her beautiful bodice embroidered with paillettes. May wearing her black beaded bandeau. May and her strikingly beautiful cape of soft orange ostrich feathers.

   Then two beautiful flower children caught my eye. Being so shy I wasnt able to ask their names. he was wearing a white suit, red shirt and a patriotic nosegay. She wore a green skirt, green embroidered silk shawl pinned around her shoulders with a gardenia. She didnt seem to be wearing anything under the shawl. But it didnt seem to matter.

   That well known PR person around town, Barry Balmer, was wearing a blue grey dinner suit with a thoroughly elegant bow tie. Barry also had his well loved silver chain around his neck. Now if only that chain could talk!

   Also seen were Claudia, Peter Couchman, Evelyn Krape, Bruce Spence, Phillipa Finney, David Williamson, the queen of the groupies, Max Spence, Brioney Behets, Geoff Manion and Marion Ballieu.
