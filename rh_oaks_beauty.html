Horses arse at
the Oaks

EVERYBODY who's anybody goes to the Melbourne cup, dont they darlings? And of course any woman who wants to be somebody goes to Oaks day later that week.

   So even though the dreary Melbourne weather looked as though it was going to turn the tap on such a dazzling occasion I headed the Lambretta towards Flemington.

   Right from the moment I swept on to the lawn with my lady friend Jennifer I realised Melbourne society people were not going to let a little cold rain spoil their parading.

   First person to catch my eagle eye was Ms William Gillespie of Bacchus Marsh who was amazingly well dressed in jockey silks. Now I've heard of people appearing in the all white colors of the club but Ms G. had a blue satin and gold horseshoe jacket which she wore with white cord tuck-in trousers and boots and a white jockey's cap.

   Preening in public certainly does call for a certain panache and I must say that Lillian Frank (compiled in collaboration with senior journalists - as they say in Melbourne's Sunday press) certainly has it. Just as we entered the members' stand Lillian passed us on the stairs. She was wearing an exquisite leopard skin coat featuring black fur trim and a matching hat. A very sensible outfit considering the weather . . . even if she did look as if she would be more at home in the cat cage at the zoo. Under the coat Lill was wearing a black jersey frock drawn together in the middle. Tres chic!

   Lovely little Delvene Delaney - who we all remember as the girl in the bikini selling Kodak film outside chemists - was the next filly to catch my eye. Didnt see her escort Strop or that boring Paul Hogan but couldnt miss D.D. She was showing off a mid length, lipstick pink suit which seemed to be symbolic of something.

   Samantha's best bet of the day went to the next person to catch her eye. Wearing a black kaftan with red tapestry sewn on to it, black boots and skivvy, a brass chain necklace and a tapestry shoulder bag, he was at least 6ft 2 inches. I would have put good money on him at ten to one just for his potential staying power. An amazing young colt.

   Even though the Oaks day is fillies day, I couldnt help seeing my old friend, Jon Finlayson. Jon was super trendy with his Zapata moustache and brown tweed cloth cap. He looked as if he was ready for a spot of "flashing", too, with a spectacular black vinyl midlength coat.
