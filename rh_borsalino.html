Samantha Dawe

Blossoms, bummies
and Borsalino

DARLINGS, I'm sure you'll all be abuzz to know that the naughty Melbourne social set is trying to ostracise little me since I told on them at the film festival.

   Dear old Roadshow and Rebelez films had me scrubbed off the list of snooty silver invitations to the Palais premiere of Borsalino & Co on sunday. But sweeties, you can't keep this Cinderella indoors when there's sparkle in the air. So I jumped the Honda and bopped down to Palais sur la mer and came down with the needful three dollars myself. Inside I found the poor common people were rudely herded into the stalls and the beautiful people (with invitations) were upstairs guzzling the champers and preening themselves in the most gauche style.

   After evading the V dashing ushers guarding the staircase I managed to elegantly intrude myself alongside Freddy and Francine Freeloader. Bliss. Gazing idly around the balcony I did notice Sharmill Films' Natalie Miller, so I hid behind a pillar - I didnt want her to see me after what I wrote about her. Then I saw David Johnston of HSV7 and I must say he does look much shorter off the box. (Pardon the expression).

   Gerald Lyons, onetime TV interviewer and talkback guru, and now PR king for the Royal Melbourne hospital looked absolutely splendid in his grey flannel suit. V. appropriate.

   ABC stalwarts Kevin Arnett and Philip Nelson looked so spiffy in the trendy's uniform of black leather jacket and chunky platform soled shoes. Whilst they were chatting away another chappie walked up. I nearly fainted when one of them said to him: "Hello Blossom, where have you been?" What a V amazing nickname!!

   One of my favorite characters for the evening was the rather large lady wearing a grey pinafore with multicolored chevron stripes on the yoke and the hem. She seemed rather keen on asian dishes, if you know what I mean darlings.

   A number of nonentities had gone to the trouble of dressing up for Borsalino & Co in blazers, straw boaters, trenchcoats and caps etc. V boring. V type.

   Colin Bennett, the feared film critic from the Age came sans his trusty gabardine raincoat. he swept into the foyer wearing dusty pink cords, baby blue skivvy and a chestnut brown jacket. Colin showed how smart he was by scorning the bourgeoisie up on the balcony. he indicated to the ushers that he preferred to sit in the stalls with the workers. Up the working class!

   Most marvellous comment on the evening was made by the woman wearing a new length skirt in muted pinks, a white sweater with 1920s ladies motif printed on the front, V long brown squashy boots and a brown tooled leather shoulder bag large enough to carry a week's tucker in. Whilst talking to her male companion she put a delicate hand to her derriere. I'm not sure whether she was checking the disposition of her unmentionables or if she imagined that one of those nasty bottom pinching Italians had got in early. I hope I see her again. She'll get more free invites.

Nation Review - july 18-24, 1975
