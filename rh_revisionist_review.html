Revisionist Review

   The book review by Michael Costigan in The Weekend Australian Review (20-21/11) does not make plain that Richard Walsh's "own recollections and linking commentary on the history of Nation Review..." given in Ferretabilia is a very lopsided one which occasionally leaves out much more than it tells.

   Following the publication of Ferretabilia by University of Queensland Press, a number of ex-staffers have become concerned about his quirky selection of pieces to reprint and the revisionist history which he has indulged in.

   In my case he has "forgotten" that I wrote the Continuity column much longer than Jeremy Salt, whom he mentions. In 1975 and until I was retrenched in December 1976, Continuity appeared under my byline.

   He also "lost" me as New Editor altogether from 1974 to 1976. Walsh writes "Leunig and Hepworth would move with Angus & Robertson to Carlton…" This is wrong and completely erases me and fellow journalist Michael Morris from being the main staffers who moved from Rosslyn St, West Melbourne to Rathdowne Street, Carlton.

   Michael Morris was maintaining a flow of features copy while I continued to write hundreds of Victorian news items and political copy, media news under the "C.M. Evans" byline, investigative journalism and the Continuity column which were all published.

   At that time, John Hepworth had already started writing his column from home in Toorak and never had an office in Carlton!

   There are a number of other factual omissions in Richard's potted "history" which will hopefully be corrected by a writer who consults the complete files of Nation Review and talks to those of us who worked there in those incredible years.

   Among the more notable articles excluded from Ferretabilia are some hilarious disclosures about the lives and shenanigans of Sir Frank Packer and his sons Kerry and Clyde.

   I'm surprised that they were not used to fill up the four pages cut during final production due to the last minute refusal of permission by garrulous "Germs" Greer.

ROBIN HOWELLS
 Nunawading, Vic

The Australian, November 29, 1993

 

Author's Note: Why and how Richard Walsh could forget my years of editorial involvement was a mystery to me.  Anyone with access to the filed copies of the publications or the microfiche compiled by the University of Queensland, can see the trail left by my byline over the years.
