Darth Carla meets Robin...

A long time ago (around 1985) in a galaxy far, far away, Darth Carla (the good and beautiful) received word that her dastardly evil twin brother Vader had sought refuge on an small insignificant blue green planet somewhere out on the western arm of the milky way, where he was posing as some guy called George Lucas and making some really fairly good movies.  Hmmmmm....

   Leaping into her luxury space capsule (without spilling a single drop of her goblet of chilled French champagne), she sped off after him, determined to bring him to Truth, Justice, and the Darth Carla way...

   Arriving at the small insignificant blue green planet, quaintly called "Earth", she landed in a somewhat messy backyard in the outer suburbs of Melbourne (Australia.  Her geography wasn't too good)  Flipping the switch on the luxury space capsule's cloaking device, she exited the vehicle just as...

   ...Winter (AKA Carla) walked out of her back door into the same outer suburban back yard.  The two stared at each other in astonishment.

   "Who are you?" they said almost in unison, whereupon they both laughed, and became best friends.

   Well, it wasn't quite like that, but close enough.

   Winter (AKA Carla) had just discovered Bulletin Boards (BBSs) and had taken to them like a duck to water, so much so in fact, that she had the dubious fame of being the only woman (in Victoria, anyway) to use Bulletin Boards on a regular basis. Like maybe three, or four or five times a day (you should have seen her phone bill!) I suppose it was inevitable that she and Robin should catch up with each other somewhere along the (modem) lines.

   Whatever. Robin (in his capacity as an inquisitive journalist) became aware of this odd person and attempted to get in touch with her.

   In those days Winter was known as "The REAL ARTICLE" because everyone on those  sorts of BBSs (read: hacker boards) used pseudonyms, and because she'd once written a "real" article on modems and BBSs that had actually been published!  Yeah, anyway, Robin sent her a message with the subject line "Desperately seeking Real" (or words to that effect), saying that he was a journalist who would like to do a story on her, etc., etc.

   I'm afraid to say that Winter didn't believe a word of it!  She honestly thought he was some little kid trying to pull her leg - Journalist indeed!  What sort of journalist who wanted to get in touch with you would use a P.O. Box contact address, anyway?!  She ignored the message.

   Time passed.. Darth Carla, Winter's alter ego, was born from a computer game she and some friends were playing. She continued to make a pest of herself on as many Bulletin Boards as she could possibly log onto by leaving terribly long and convoluted messages (like this essay) on all of them. One Sysop (Vegemite sandwich, anyone?) in sheer self defence told her she should start her own Bulletin Board.  She did.  It was called "The REAL CONNECTION (the REAL world starts here!)" and it was very popular.

   Again Robin attempted to interest her in having an article written about her exploits.  No dice.  Laughingly she told some young friends who happened to be visiting her BBS about this weird person who claimed to be a journalist.  They looked at each other. "Robin?" said one "Oh we know him!  He is a journalist! He goes to the same computer club that we go to!"

   Shame! Mortification! Red faced... So at long last Winter got in touch with Robin, who did try to do an article on her and her Bulletin Board, but it was too late... the magazine closed just before the article was to go to print, so I guess you all miss out on the Grand Story.  Sorry. Mea culpa.

   Nevertheless, Winter, her young boyfriend Julian (AKA Deep Image), and Robin and his family became close friends, and have been ever since. Winter ended up marrying her young boyfriend (twenty years her junior) and this year will mark their twelfth wedding anniversary. With the advent of the Internet and the WWW, Bulletin Boards lost popularity and so after eleven years, The REAL CONNECTION (later known as "death... by chocolate") closed down. We had a wake. Now Julian is a computer systems engineer and unix guru and Winter is a web designer and web graphics nut at Digital Tapestries (see below) who happens to enjoy the boredom of transcription and keyboarding, and Robin decided to go public and set up a web site of his own....

   Which is how yours truly got to be doing the web authoring for Robin's site!
