So, who IS Robin Howells?

Robin has worked for more than 45 years in the Communications business including extensive experience in Journalism in daily newspapers and magazines, Public Relations, Broadcasting, and Advertising. He held senior executive positions at The Herald, The Age, Newsday, Nation Review and the Victorian Farmer and was Victorian Manager of Australia’s first national media representation company, which represented Press, Radio and Television in all States.

Highlights of his career include:

He was made a Fellow of the Advertising Institute of Australia (the highest membership ranking) in the 1960s. Also holds the Associate and Licenciate Diploma from the A.I.A.

During the 1970s, whilst News Editor of Nation Review  when it was Australia's leading weekly news magazine, he honed his skills as an investigative journalist. During this time he worked with Richard Walsh, Mungo MacCallum, John Hepworth, Bob Ellis, Richard Neville, Patrick Cooke and Michael Leunig.

Immediately after being appointed Editor of the Victorian Farmer  he carried out a complete redesign of the publication to improve its readability and usefulness for Victoria's farmers. Awarded Best Magazine Feature Story prize for an article on the Royal Melbourne Show by the Royal Agricultural Society in 1978

Planned and did all the day-to-day work on the Anti-Cancer Council of Victoria's (ACCV) first big Slip, Slop, Slap campaign's Public Relations in 1981. Carried out other campaigns for the ACCV including their Melbourne Cup Day Appeal.

An article The girl who played hookey, originally published in The Age, April 10, 1981 was chosen for reprinting in The Best of The Age 1980-81 edited by Cameron Forbes and published by Nelson.

He has been using IBM PC compatible computers since 1982. For many years he wrote on computer topics for Computing Australia , PC Australia , Australian Personal Computer and Today's Computer magazines. Since the growth of the Internet and the World Wide Web he has written about interesting sites for the SYTE section of The Australian, The Big Issue and PC Update - the monthly magazine of Melbourne PC User Group Inc. Wrote and presented a series of talks for broadcast on shortwave by Radio Australia, which extended over 18 months, on the uses of computers by shortwave and DX listeners.

In August 1988 he set up a closed system computerised Bulletin Board System (BBS) for use by fellow members of the Australian Journalists' Association. In May and June 1989, as a personal contribution to Radio Australia's 50th Anniversary celebrations, he put online the MATILDA BBS running 24 hours a day for the use of overseas listeners to the station. In 1990 and 1991 he ran the WYSIWYG BBS, which was aimed at desktop publishing users.

Before retiring in 1998 he had been a member of the Australian Journalists' Association section of the Media, Entertainment and Arts Alliance and the Melbourne Press Club for nearly 25 years.
