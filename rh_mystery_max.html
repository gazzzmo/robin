Mystery Max throws
   up questions on ASIO

           By WILLIAM H. MARTIN       

ONE OF this country's most incredible spy stories broke last weekend when Melbourne's Sunday observer carried headlines screaming: ASIO's top agent exposes our amazing world of sex, treachery and terror.

   This alleged top spy is 25 year old Czech born Maxmilian Weschler who says that he reached the exalted position in ASIO in 26 months and was being paid the amazing salary of $100 cash a week by them in addition to receiving $36 weekly in commonwealth sickness benefits.

   How Mystery Max came to receive sickness benefits and what Max's sickness is are questions receiving scrutiny from people who've known Max and his medical history. The disclosures are expected to throw new light on the calibre of the informers and agents employed by ASIO.

   Max's deluded, dubious claim to fame is that he "infiltrated" two perfectly legal organisations - the Socialist Workers League and the Socialist Youth Alliance. As these two groups pointed out after Maxie's mumblings, they are both legal organisations; they campaign openly to win mass acceptance of their socialist aims; their objectives are openly stated in their publications such as the fortnightly Direct action.

   What Mystery Max did do was to disclose that ASIO has continued to carry out harassment and secret surveillance of legal political organisations. ASIO filmed and spied on members of these groups. They also tapped the phone of the SWL Melbourne office.

   Maxie's standard of reporting to ASIO shows how unreliable most of their information could be. He said that the catholic church was the unwitting host to SWL members at its Ocean Grove complex. Yet this camp is owned by the methodists!

   He also claims to have used sex as a weapon in his "spying" adventures. Those who've known him well in the 

past 12 months are mildly surprised by this disclosure.

   A number of questions remain unanswered about Mystery Maxie.

How could he afford to move into the Centre City Club in Flinders street, Melbourne and pay $37 weekly rent from december 5 last until february 19?

Where did he obtain the money to buy a color TV? When questioned about it by SWL members he said his aunt was feeling sorry for him and gave him the money to buy it. He has since admitted that another story about his aunt giving him money was a lie.

Was it coincidence that he was at the City Centre Club at the same time as runaway British MP John Stonehouse? In 1969 a Czech security agent  Joseph Frolik  defected to the Americans and alleged that Stonehouse was a Czech agent. This was investigated by British security service D15. They later reported to Harold Wilson that Frolik's allegations were without foundation

How did Mystery Max become naturalised in less than the three years residence required to qualify? In one article he states he arrived in Australia on june 7, 1969, in another he states he was naturalised in late 1971. At press time the immigration department in Canberra had not been able to answer this paradox.

Was Mystery Max selected to come to Australia after the Russian invasion of Czechoslovakia by an ASIO agent in Vienna who was posing as a migrant selection officer?

His limited command of the English language and his inability to understand questions and answer them was shown on A Current Affair last Monday. How could this man report accurately and faithfully to his ASIO puppeteers?

NATION REVIEW - february 28 - march 6, 1975 - Page 517
