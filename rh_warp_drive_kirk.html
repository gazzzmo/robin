BBS FILE l ROBIN HOWELLS

Warp drive Mr Kirk

It is late at night. The reports have started to come in of mysterious sightings of an exciting BBS seen hovering over Victoria. Triffid and I enter the Scitec time capsule with trepidation... we are about to enter The Outer Limits.

   Faster than the speed of light we are there. Captain Kirk greets us in a strange language. His space craft takes control of my monitor. . . I have lost control. Triffid squeals with delight.

   Captain Kirk (Peter Dorrell) started the Outer Limits BBS on September 1. It is dedicated to science fiction, fantasy and horror.

   "The name of the BBS and the opening logo of the 'ENTERPRISE' come from the television show from the sixties, Star Trek, which happens to be my favorite," Dorrell says. "I thought there was a need for something different in the way of BBS and having a great love for S.F. and computers, I decided to join the two together and share it with others."

   There are about 150 users and around 60 of these log on regularly. Only three per cent are women. On average, there are 10 callers a day and about 40 at weekends. Most people spend about 40 minutes on the board.

   The message areas are alive with discussion on science fiction, fantasy, television and books. In message area two, Captain Kirk gives details of new film releases coming up and such things as Video Top 20 and what is "watchable" on television this week.

   Students or hobbyists, who are into sci-fi, either through books, film or fantasy role playing games, are the big contributors with messages coming from such characters as Mithrandir Grey, The Griblet, Zaphod, Wenlock Burton, Gandalf the-grey, Vivamort, Admiral Korg and Proton. If you are interested in books like Chapter House of Dune, authors like Isaac Asimov, E.E. "Doc" Smith, Michael Moorcock, Douglas Adams, Robert Heinlen, Larry Niven, H.P. Lovecraft, or Philip Jose Farmer then you had better visit The Outer Limits soon.

   "I think BBSs are going to get bigger and better in the near future," Dorrell says. "However, they will diversify into different interest groups, unlike what they are now. At the moment BBSs are used just to exchange computer related information, but I think people will turn to them for entertainment such as on-line games, multi-user games etc."

   He works at a window manufacturers and his work does not include the use of computers, which are only a hobby for him. In his spare time he enjoys watching a good science fiction film, or reading a book.

   One of the most popular sections of The Outer Limits is the serialised story called, "Just Imagine Jeanie". New episodes are added at regular intervals. 

This is the first of many stories planned for the BBS. The first episodes can be found in the IBMPC file section; just type "T" from the file menu, then type "JEANIE.TXT", and the first episodes will print out.

   Here is a quick guide to travelling The Outer Limits. From the main menu.

(TYPE M): Message area to read about new film releases and general information.
(TYPE A): Try your sci-fi knowledge with the trivia quiz; there is a prize to be won every month.
(TYPE E): Read episodes of Just Imagine Jeanie.
(TYPE F): Download science fiction programs.
(TYPE Y): Communicate with Captain Kirk.

   The sci-fi trivia quiz and the serialised stories are both very popular. Dorrell is hoping to expand these areas soon and also plans to have on-line games, including adventures to play. A downloadable manual is not available.

 Suprtrek.ARC a color version of a great classic.

   The Outer Limits BBS hardware is a STAR IBM clone with 640K of RAM, four floppy drives, multi-function card, color graphics card, games port, Sakada amber monitor, Olympia NP printer, NetComm 21/23a Smartmodem and coming soon, a 20M hard disk. Dorrell estimates it has cost around $2500 to set up the BBS.
